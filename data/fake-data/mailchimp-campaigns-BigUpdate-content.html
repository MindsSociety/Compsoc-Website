Hi everyone,

We've got some big announcements for this week starting with events:

Google HashCode Qualification Round

This year Minds will be hosting the Google Hash Code Qualification Round this year in the Callan building (lab 4).
Hash Code is open to university students and industry professionals (18+) in Europe, the Middle East and Africa. Participants register and compete in teams of two to four. Registration is free and will close on 9th February 2016 at 18:00 CET.

Signup form.

When signing up, make sure to select our hub: "Ireland - Maynooth University (Maynooth)" from the hub selector.
Once you have signed up, you will need to join a team; to do so go to the Hash Code Judge and build your team. If you don't have a team make sure to ask around in our Facebook group.

Timetable for HashCode

  • 17:00 - Set up in Callan - Showing the Livestream
  • 17:30 - Task is announced
  • 17:45 - Competition starts
  • 20:30 - Scoreboard freezes
  • 21:30 - Times up!

Omega Kiss week Inter-Soc Event

For KISS week we will be showing Scott Pilgrim Vs The World on Monday at 6:30pm in Iontas. There will be buckets on the door for Dublin Rape Crisis Center and movie snacks in the form of popcorn.


Name Change
Following discussions and a vote in our previous AGM, we will be changing the society's name! After taking input from our members (and instantly regretting it) the committee have chosen a new name from the suggestions. From Feb 15th we will be changing from Minds to CompSoc!


Meetings on a Thursday
Starting from Thursday Feb 18th, Minds will be held in CB1 in the Callan building every Thursday of term.

If you want to keep up to date add our calendar to your devices

  • HTML
  • ICAL (Download the .ics file and add it to your calendar, I wish Google would make this easier, see here)

Problem of the Week
In the interest of keeping a competitive feel in the society, we will be posting a new problem or two every week for our members to solve. At the start of each Thursday meeting we will spend 10-15 mins going through the solution, starting on our first meeting in CB1 (after Hash Code). To start this off we will run a sample question on Thursday Feb 

Submissions can be done in any language, more details to follow when we have the infrastructure sorted out.
